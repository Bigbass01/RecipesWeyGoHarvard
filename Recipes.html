Classic Spaghetti

This Classic Spaghetti recipe is the ultimate comfort food — simple, rich, and bursting with flavor. Made with perfectly cooked pasta, a robust homemade tomato sauce, and a sprinkle of parmesan, it’s a timeless dish that never fails to satisfy. Whether you're cooking for family or impressing guests, this spaghetti brings the vibes and taste wey go Harvard!

How to make a classic Italian Spaghetti wey go Harvard

Making a classic Italian spaghetti is easier than you think, and it starts with fresh ingredients and a bit of patience. Begin by boiling your pasta in salted water until al dente. While that's cooking, heat olive oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onions until fragrant. Add crushed tomatoes, a pinch of sugar, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper, then let it simmer gently to deepen the flavor. Toss the drained spaghetti into the sauce and let it absorb the goodness for a few minutes. Finish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped parsley for that authentic Italian touch. Simple, rich, and comforting. Watch the video below.

Fluffy Pancakes

These Fluffy Pancakes are light, buttery, and melt-in-your-mouth delicious. Perfect for lazy weekend mornings or a quick breakfast fix, they rise beautifully and soak up syrup like a dream. Serve with fresh fruits, syrup, or a dollop of whipped cream for that Harvard-level breakfast experience.

How to make Fluffy Pancakes wey go Harvard

For irresistibly fluffy pancakes wey go Harvard, start by whisking together your dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. In a separate bowl, beat eggs, then add milk, melted butter, and a splash of vanilla extract. Combine the wet and dry ingredients gently — don't overmix; a few lumps are fine! Let the batter rest for about 5–10 minutes to activate the baking powder. Heat a lightly greased pan over medium heat and pour in the batter using a ladle or measuring cup. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden. Stack high, drizzle with syrup or honey, and enjoy your cloud-like pancakes! Watch the video below.

Fresh Garden Salad

This Fresh Garden Salad is crisp, colorful, and loaded with goodness. A refreshing mix of lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, carrots, and a tangy homemade vinaigrette makes it the perfect side or light main dish. Healthy no mean boring — this salad na vibe wey dey nourish and refresh.

How to make Fresh Garden Salad wey go Harvard

To create a fresh garden salad wey go Harvard, start with a base of mixed greens like romaine, spinach, or arugula. Add a variety of colorful veggies such as cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, and shredded carrots for crunch and flavor. For added protein, toss in some chickpeas or grilled chicken. Drizzle with a simple vinaigrette made from olive oil, balsamic vinegar, mustard, honey, salt, and pepper. Toss everything together gently and serve chilled for a refreshing side dish or light meal.
